Submarine cables play an important role in connecting networks between continents. The first submarine communications cable was laid across the Rhine River in 1854, connecting countries between England and France. As of 2025,more than 600 submarine cables are deployed worldwide, carrying over 95% international data traffic.

The cases of Submarine Cable destroy by Ship Vessel

Despite their importance in global connection,submarine cables are vulnerable to physical damage, particularly from human activities such as fishing, anchoring, and ship movements. According to the International Cable Protection Committee (ICPC), an estimated 100 to 200 incidents of damage to undersea cables are reported worldwide each year. The most common cause of these breaks is accidental interference from ship anchors and trawling gear.

While submarine cables are engineered to be durable and often buried beneath the seabed in shallow waters, they are not immune to disruption.70-80 per cent of the damage of submarine cable is reported to be the caused of ship anchor dragging. As noted by TeleGeography, some cable routes are repeatedly affected due to their proximity to heavily trafficked maritime paths.
