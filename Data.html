Introduction Web Crawling Results About Me

Kernel Density Estimation

After crawling a total of 70,000 vessel location records over a period of three weeks, I estimated ship movements by connecting individual points to reconstruct maritime routes. Kernel Density Estimation (KDE) was then applied to determine the density of these maritime paths.

Subsequently, by performing a spatial intersection between the vessel density and submarine cable locations, we identified segments of submarine cables that are highly correlated with vessel routes.

Understanding the relationship between vessel routing and submarine cables provides valuable insights into the vulnerability of these cables. Based on this information, we can simulate scenarios such as accidental damage to specific submarine cables caused by ship anchors. This allows us to assess the potential impact on the overall cable network.

Betweenness Centrality Analysis

To calculate the importance of submarine cables, I used the method of Betweenness Centrality (BC).

First, in an ideal scenario where all submarine cables function properly, I calculated the importance of each cable within the network. The cables named 2Africa, Medusa Submarine Cable System, Havfrue/AEC-2, Confluence-1, and South America-1 (SAM-1) were identified as the top five most important cables under this scenario. What if the submarine cables are intentionally destroyed?

What if the 10 most important submarine cables were intentionally destroyed? What would happen to the rest of the network? To simulate a scenario in which these key cables are targeted, I removed the top 10 cables identified earlier and analyzed the network again. This allowed me to observe which other cables become important and take on a critical substitutive role.

The cables Europe-India Gateway (EIG), South American Crossing (SAC), Bifrost, Pan-American Crossing (PAC), and Apricot emerged as the top five candidates for substitution in the event of cable being intentionally destroyed.

The Vulnerability of Submarine Cables

Finally, by simulating the destruction of submarine cables based on Kernel Density Estimation (KDE) results, cable segments with high KDE values (above 80%) were removed. This approach helps identify which cables are both critical and potentially more secure.

The results highlight the following cables as particularly important and resilient: 2Africa, Medusa Submarine Cable System, FLAG Europe-Asia (FEA), EllaLink, and South America-1 (SAM-1).

Conclusion
